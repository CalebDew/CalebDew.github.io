What is the purpose of the BYU education

According to the class:

  • "the focus in learning is not to become the best of all. We are learning so we can go out and serve. We are learning to we can go out and learn. I sometimes think down the roads I could go and realize, what would be the point of getting a hire level job and making money? Well, there really is no reason to make money if you're not gonna spend it. What I mean by that is we are to make money to contribute to society, whether that's be circulating it back into the economy or helping out your kids, or giving to charities, or using it to learning new things."

  • "At college, we're not just learning the things we need to pass, we should be learning how to think about important things. And that involves the study of things we might not find worthwhile at first glance. A broad education will teach us to be better at thinking broadly."

  • "I love that BYU encourages that and helps us come closer to God as well as strengthen our testimonies. It's a blessing that we are able to learn and expand our knowledge in a setting that supports us as we strive to develop a relationship with God."

Why should you attend BYU?

According to the class:

  • To be an academic anablep in Dr. Brinton's context means to see and understand things from not only an academic point of view but also a religious point of view. While working as a speech language pathologist, Dr. Brinton learned to use her beliefs and values in a way to influence her patients' lives for the better. Not only was she able to do this through her vast knowledge as a speech pathologist, but she was also able to use spiritual insight to frame her work. In one of her examples, she worked with a child who had very limited language development by giving him a journal. This approach allowed him to connect with his family, recognize his self-worth and greatly enhance his ability to communicate. Overall, her work with this child was unconventional and it didn't simply just help him with his language development. Similarly, studying at BYU doesn't simply just give me an education. It gives me an eternal, celestial perspective on the world. It gives meaning to my studies, and ties in what I believe as a disciple of Christ to my career.

  • To be an academic anablep is to be able to see both sides of an issue, both through a spiritual and a traditional framework. I love this example that was given because it showed how seeing both frameworks and creating a synthetic blend of both led to the best results for the child. I think BYU uniquely can help us to develop this type of outlook on the world through presenting scholarly information with a spiritual overlay. I feel like my experience at BYU so far has helped me to learn by study and by faith, which helps me to see both sides of an issue and make decisions on the best way to approach it.

  • I think that being an academic anablep is a great analogy having your eyes set on goals above and below the water. Being able to see the world through a celestial and spiritual view and understand how that ties into the secular world will help accomplish your goals and plans with more ease. I think that BYU is the best way to get the anablep perspective. We are taught subjects and fields that everyone has the opportunity to learn but we also know how to rely on God and have Him in our lives. Because of the view BYU gives me I know that I can learn and continue progressing outside of college and outside of this earthly life. I am able to worry less because I have an eternal perspective and I understand that what happens here on earth is not the end.
